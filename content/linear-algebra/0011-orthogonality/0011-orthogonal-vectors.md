# Orthogonal vectors

A pair of vectors, $u$ and $v$, from $\mathbb{C}^{m}$ are orthogonal if their inner product is zero, that is, $⟨u, v⟩ = 0$.

## Example

The vectors

$$
u = \begin{bmatrix}
2 + 3i\\
4 − 2i\\
1 + i\\
1 + i
\end{bmatrix}
\\;\\;\\;
v = \begin{bmatrix}
1 − i\\
2 + 3i\\
4 − 6i\\
1
\end{bmatrix}
$$
 
are orthogonal since

$$
⟨u, v⟩ = (2 − 3i)(1 − i) + (4 + 2i)(2 + 3i) + (1 − i)(4 − 6i) + (1 − i)(1) = (−1 −5i) + (2 + 16i) + (−2 − 10i) + (1 − i) = 0 + 0i
$$
